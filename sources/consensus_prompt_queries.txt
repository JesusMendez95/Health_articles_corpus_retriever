Psychology
 1 — Psychological interventions for general well-being
What are the most effective evidence-based psychological interventions — including cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT), dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) skills training, positive psychology interventions, and mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) — for improving emotional regulation, psychological resilience, stress management, and overall subjective well-being in healthy adults without clinical mental health diagnoses? Include studies that measure outcomes such as self-reported well-being, perceived stress, life satisfaction, and emotional stability. Prioritize randomized controlled trials, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses published in peer-reviewed journals.

 2 — Psychology applied to modern challenges
How do evidence-based psychological strategies — such as cognitive restructuring, behavioral activation, self-compassion training, acceptance-based approaches, habit formation techniques, and values-based goal setting — help non-clinical adult populations manage modern everyday challenges including chronic stress, procrastination, decision fatigue, information overload, social media-related anxiety, work-life balance difficulties, and relationship conflicts? What measurable improvements in daily functioning, productivity, interpersonal relationships, and emotional well-being have been documented in the research literature?

 3 — Preventive psychology and personal development
What preventive psychology programs and personal development interventions — including resilience training, emotional intelligence development, growth mindset cultivation, strengths-based approaches, and self-determination theory applications — have demonstrated effectiveness in promoting long-term psychological well-being, preventing burnout, and enhancing adaptive coping mechanisms in healthy adult populations across diverse cultural contexts? What are the most practical, scalable formats for delivering these interventions (individual, group, digital, self-guided)?

Psychiatry
 1 — Preventive and lifestyle psychiatry

What are the recommended dosage, frequency, and duration for each intervention to achieve statistically significant improvements in mental health outcomes?

 2 — Psychiatric neurobiology applied to everyday life
How do current psychiatric research findings on neurotransmitter systems (serotonin, dopamine, GABA, norepinephrine), the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, neuroinflammation, and the gut-brain axis inform practical, non-pharmacological strategies that the general adult population can implement to maintain emotional stability, regulate mood, improve sleep quality, and prevent the development of stress-related psychiatric symptoms? What lifestyle modifications have the strongest neurobiological evidence base?

 3 — Psychoeducation and early detection
How effective are psychoeducation programs, mental health literacy campaigns, and early psychiatric screening tools in improving mental health awareness, reducing stigma, promoting help-seeking behavior, and identifying subclinical psychological distress in the general adult population? What delivery formats (workplace programs, digital platforms, community settings, primary care integration) have demonstrated the best outcomes in terms of reach, engagement, and measurable mental health improvements?

Psychoanalysis
 1 — Evidence of psychoanalysis in everyday life
What is the empirical evidence for the effectiveness of psychoanalytic and psychodynamic concepts — including defense mechanism awareness, unconscious process recognition, transference patterns, attachment theory applications, mentalization, and reflective functioning — in improving self-awareness, emotional regulation, interpersonal relationship quality, and overall psychological well-being in non-clinical adult populations? How do these psychoanalytic insights compare to other therapeutic approaches in terms of long-term sustained benefits and depth of personality change?

 2 — Self-Applied Psychoanalytic Practices
How do self-applied psychoanalytically informed practices — such as structured self-reflection journaling, free association writing, dream analysis and interpretation, exploring personal narrative patterns, identifying recurring relational dynamics, and examining emotional reactions to daily events — impact self-understanding, emotional processing, anxiety reduction, and personal growth in healthy adults who are not engaged in formal psychotherapy? What frameworks and structured protocols exist for implementing these practices independently?

 3 — Contemporary psychoanalysis and its applications
What are the practical, real-world applications of contemporary psychoanalytic theories — including object relations theory, self-psychology, relational psychoanalysis, attachment-based psychodynamic approaches, and intersubjective systems theory — for understanding and improving everyday emotional experiences, workplace dynamics, parenting practices, romantic relationships, and self-development in the general non-clinical population? What formats (psychoanalytic psychoeducation, group reflection, bibliotherapy, online programs) have been studied for making psychoanalytic knowledge accessible to lay populations?

Neuroscience
 1 — Neuroplasticity and cognitive-emotional training
What evidence-based neuroscience interventions leveraging neuroplasticity — including cognitive training programs, mindfulness meditation and its effects on prefrontal cortex and amygdala function, learning new complex skills (music, language, dance), aerobic exercise-induced brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) production, and deliberate practice paradigms — are most effective for improving emotional regulation, cognitive flexibility, attention, working memory, and stress resilience in healthy adults? What are the optimal protocols (duration, frequency, intensity) demonstrated by neuroimaging and behavioral outcome studies?

 2 — Neuroscience of stress and emotional regulation
How do current neuroscience findings on the stress response system — including the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis, autonomic nervous system regulation, vagal tone, cortisol rhythms, amygdala reactivity, and prefrontal cortex modulation — inform practical, accessible strategies (such as diaphragmatic breathing, heart rate variability biofeedback, cold water exposure, progressive muscle relaxation, and polyvagal-informed exercises) for improving emotional stability, reducing chronic stress effects, and building physiological resilience in the general adult population?

 3 — Neuroscience of Sleep, Nutrition, and Performance
What does the neuroscience literature reveal about the impact of sleep architecture optimization, chrononutrition, omega-3 fatty acid supplementation, polyphenol-rich diets, intermittent fasting, hydration, and microbiome diversity on brain health, cognitive performance (memory consolidation, executive function, creativity), emotional regulation, and neuroprotection in healthy adults aged 20-55? What are the most actionable, evidence-based recommendations with the strongest effect sizes for translating these neuroscience findings into daily life improvements?

 Neuropsychology
 1 — Cognitive optimization in healthy adults
What neuropsychological strategies, cognitive training programs, and brain-based interventions — including computerized cognitive training (working memory training, attention training, speed of processing training), metacognitive strategy training, spaced retrieval practice, dual n-back tasks, and cognitive remediation approaches — are most effective for optimizing memory, attention, executive functioning, processing speed, and cognitive flexibility in healthy adults without neurological or psychiatric conditions? What is the evidence for transfer effects from training tasks to real-world cognitive performance and daily functioning?

 2 — Neuropsychological assessment for self-awareness
How can neuropsychological assessment frameworks and cognitive profiling — including evaluation of attention subtypes, memory systems (episodic, semantic, procedural, working memory), executive function domains (planning, inhibition, cognitive flexibility, decision-making), visuospatial abilities, and processing speed — be adapted and applied in non-clinical settings to help healthy adults understand their cognitive strengths and weaknesses, optimize their learning strategies, improve workplace performance, and make more informed lifestyle and career decisions?

 3 — Cognitive reserve and prevention of decline
What is the current evidence on cognitive reserve, brain maintenance, and neuroprotective lifestyle factors — including education, bilingualism, complex occupational demands, physical exercise, social engagement, cognitively stimulating leisure activities, musical training, and meditation — in slowing age-related cognitive decline and maintaining neuropsychological functioning in healthy adults across the lifespan? What practical recommendations do longitudinal studies support for building and maintaining cognitive reserve starting in early and middle adulthood?

Neuropsychiatry
 1 — Functional symptoms and somatization
What does the neuropsychiatric literature reveal about the neurobiology, prevalence, assessment, and evidence-based non-pharmacological treatment of functional neurological and psychiatric symptoms — including psychosomatic complaints, chronic unexplained fatigue, brain fog, tension headaches, functional dizziness, irritable bowel syndrome, non-cardiac chest pain, and stress-related somatic symptoms — in adults without established psychiatric or neurological diagnoses? What integrated neuropsychiatric approaches combining neurological and psychological interventions have demonstrated effectiveness in symptom reduction and improved quality of life?

 2 — Brain-behavior interface and emotional stability
How do neuropsychiatric findings on brain-behavior relationships — including prefrontal cortex function and impulse control, limbic system regulation and emotional reactivity, basal ganglia circuits and motivation/habit formation, default mode network activity and rumination, and neurotransmitter system balance — inform practical, accessible interventions for improving emotional stability, behavioral self-regulation, impulse control, and adaptive stress responses in the general adult population without psychiatric diagnoses? What non-invasive neuromodulation techniques (tDCS, neurofeedback, biofeedback) have shown promise for these purposes?

 3 — Neuropsychiatric impact of modern lifestyle
What is the neuropsychiatric evidence for the impact of modern lifestyle factors — including chronic sleep deprivation, sedentary behavior, ultra-processed food consumption, digital screen overexposure, social isolation, chronic low-grade inflammation, environmental toxin exposure, and circadian rhythm disruption — on brain structure, neurotransmitter function, cognitive performance, and emotional health in the general population? What comprehensive lifestyle modification programs based on neuropsychiatric evidence have demonstrated measurable improvements in both neurological and psychological health markers?

Functional Medicine
 1 — Gut-brain axis and mental health
What is the evidence from functional medicine and integrative health research for the role of the gut-brain axis, gut microbiome composition and diversity, intestinal permeability (‘leaky gut’), probiotics, prebiotics, synbiotics, and targeted dietary interventions (elimination diets, Mediterranean diet, anti-inflammatory protocols) in modulating mood, anxiety levels, cognitive function, emotional resilience, and overall mental health in non-clinical adult populations? What specific probiotic strains, dietary protocols, and supplementation regimens have demonstrated the strongest evidence for psychobiotic effects?

 2 — Nutritional deficiencies and cognitive-emotional health
How do functional medicine approaches to identifying and correcting nutritional deficiencies and metabolic imbalances — including vitamin D, magnesium, zinc, B-complex vitamins (B6, B9 folate, B12), iron, omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA), amino acid precursors (tryptophan, tyrosine), and antioxidant status — impact mental health symptoms (anxiety, low mood, irritability, cognitive fog, fatigue), emotional stability, cognitive performance, and overall quality of life in adults without psychiatric diagnoses? What biomarkers and functional testing approaches are most useful for personalized nutritional interventions?

 3 — Integrative functional medicine protocols
What are the clinical outcomes of comprehensive functional medicine protocols — integrating personalized nutrition plans, targeted supplementation based on laboratory testing, gut microbiome restoration, hormonal optimization (cortisol, thyroid, sex hormones), detoxification support, mitochondrial function enhancement, sleep architecture optimization, stress management techniques, and movement prescriptions — for improving energy levels, mental clarity, emotional well-being, resilience to stress, and overall quality of life in healthy adults seeking health optimization rather than disease treatment? What evidence supports the functional medicine model of addressing root causes versus symptom management for subclinical mental health concerns?

 1 — Mindfulness-Based Interventions for Emotional Well-Being
What are the most effective mindfulness-based interventions — including Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR), Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT), brief mindfulness training programs, loving-kindness meditation (LKM), compassion-focused meditation, and open monitoring meditation — for improving emotional regulation, reducing perceived stress, enhancing psychological resilience, and increasing overall subjective well-being in healthy adults without clinical mental health diagnoses? What are the optimal duration, frequency, and format (in-person, app-based, self-guided) of practice required to produce significant and sustained improvements? Prioritize randomized controlled trials, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses.

 2 — Neuroscience of Mindfulness and Meditation

How do these neuroplastic changes correlate with measurable improvements in attention, emotional regulation, stress resilience, empathy, and cognitive flexibility? What minimum practice duration and frequency are associated with detectable neurological changes in neuroimaging studies?

 3 — Mindfulness for Modern Lifestyle Challenges
How do mindfulness and contemplative science practices — including body scan meditation, breath awareness, mindful eating, walking meditation, digital mindfulness interventions, and informal mindfulness integration into daily routines — help non-clinical adult populations manage modern lifestyle challenges such as chronic stress, attention fragmentation, digital overload, sleep disturbances, emotional reactivity, workplace burnout, and interpersonal difficulties? What are the comparative effectiveness, adherence rates, and long-term outcomes of app-based mindfulness programs (Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer, Waking Up) versus traditional instructor-led mindfulness training in community and workplace settings?
